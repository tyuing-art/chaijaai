History of Chai Jaai

Journey through time and discover how the ancient art of tea-making evolved into the modern Chai Jaai experience

🏛️

The Foundation

Founded as a harmonious blend of British tea room elegance and authentic Kashmiri artistry, Chai Jaai represents the perfect fusion of two rich cultural traditions.

🏞️

The Location

Nestled on the historic Bund by the serene Jhelum river in Srinagar, housed within the artistic Mahattas Studio, offering breathtaking views of Kashmir's natural beauty.

🎨

The Artistry

Our décor features Victorian touches, traditional samovars, exquisite naqashi art, and authentic papier-mâché elements, creating an atmosphere of timeless elegance.

🏛️ Ancient Times
🍃 Tea arrives in Kashmir via the legendary Silk Road from China and Central Asia, weaving itself into the very fabric of local culture and daily life.
👑 17th–18th Century
🌸 The magnificent Mughal empire brings refined tea traditions to Kashmir. Kahwa with its golden saffron and Noon Chai with its distinctive pink hue become beloved Kashmiri staples.
🫖 1800s
✨ The elegant Samovar (Russian kettle) and delicate bone china cups become symbols of sophistication, elevating tea service to an art form in Kashmiri households.
🎯 2016
🏠 Chai Jaai is lovingly founded in the heart of Srinagar by visionary Muneer Kabir, creating a magical fusion of traditional Kashmiri hospitality and elegant English tea room culture.
🌟 2017–2024
🏆 Chai Jaai blossoms into a beloved cultural icon, renowned for its enchanting ambiance, exquisite artisan teaware, and an impressive collection of 60+ premium tea varieties from around the world.
